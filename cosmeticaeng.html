Hemp for the production of Cosmetics

The use of hemp derivatives for cosmetic formulation dates back to ancient of history. It is known, for example, that in ancient China the preparations Cannabis topicals were used externally to treat skin rashes, ulcers, wounds and for alopecia. There is also archaeological evidence indicating that topical cannabis preparations were used by the Egyptians to treat eye problems and for wound dressing. The medieval Arabs applied their leaves to improve some skin conditions such as pityriasis and lichen planus. In more modern times, already in the 20th century, and before the prohibition of its marketing, cannabis tinctures were frequently sold at any pharmacy for the treatment of calluses, irritable bladder and pain menstrual. Thus, we can verify that the therapeutic potential of this plant offers a wide range of possibilities. To better understand the process by which cannabis derivatives can act on the skin, it must be understood that it has receptors for substances endocannabinoids that our own body synthesizes in what makes up a system endocannabinoid (internally synthesized), which is involved in such complex systems such as the development of the epidermis and its appendages (hair and nails). This system endocannabinoid is also involved in cell survival, in responses immune and inflammatory, in the synthesis of dermal lipids or in the transmission of Sensory stimuli to the Central Nervous System (CNS). Numerous clinical studies are currently being carried out where there is still reveals the potential use of cannabis and its derivatives as ingredients essential in the treatment of some dermal imbalances such as atopic dermatitis, scleroderma or psoriasis.
