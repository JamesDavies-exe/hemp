The cannabinoid CBD is a non-psychoactive isomer of tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), its The most growing available form is CBD oil. By not having psychoactive activity offered as a totally safe asset. On a practical level, CBD hemp oil we could say that it is nothing more than a concentrated solvent extract composed of flowers or cannabis leaves that are dissolved in a vegetable oil such as sunflower oil, hemp oil or olive oil. The solvents used for its extraction can vary from relatively harmless organic solvents, such as ethanol or isopropyl alcohol, others more toxic such as petroleum ether. Its extraction can also be done with supercritical fluids such as butane or CO2. The exact conditions and solvents applied for its manufacture have a great impact on, for example, the taste, the color and the viscosity of the final product. Because many other components of the plant are extracted together with the desired cannabinoids, sometimes these must be eliminated, in order to obtain a purer final product. Its use is mainly therapeutic, both oral and topical. Remembering what our tutor María José Gea explained to us about CBD, we know that In Europe, the cultivation of the hemp variety of Cannabis sativa is allowed as long as when the THC concentration does not exceed 2%. On this basis, in the Convention Single Law on Narcotic Drugs (signed in New York on March 30, 1961) is considered narcotic the resins, the extracts and the tincture of the Cannabis. In Annex II of the Regulation (EC) 1223/2009, which lists the substances whose use is prohibited narcotics (entry 306) appear as prohibited substances. Therefore, the cannabis, its resin, extracts and tinctures could not be used in cosmetics. Nevertheless, are excluded from the classification as narcotic drugs the seeds and leaves of hemp, which do not contain cannabidiol. In order to use it as a cosmetic ingredient, what the industry has done is obtain it synthetically, this is a way of ensure that the ingredient complies with current legislation.
